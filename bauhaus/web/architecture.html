1925

The Bauhaus moves to Dessau,where Gropius designs the Bauhaus studio building.

Marcel Breuer returns to Bauhaus as a master and designs an early version of the bent metal Club chair (model B3)

1927

Marcel Breuer designs interiors for the Weissenhofsiedlung housing project.

1929

Ludwig Mies van der Rohe designs the Barcelona Pavilion.

1930

Mies van der Rohe becomes the last director of the Bauhaus and designs Villa Tugendhat in Czech Republic defining the functionalist style.

1934

Walter Gropius moves to London,and lives in the Isokon building designed by Wells Coates.

1937

Gropius moves to US and designs Gropius House in Lincoln,Massachusetts.

Breuer moves to the Harvard Graduate School of Design to work with Gropius.

Moholy-Nagy goes to Chicago,founding the New Bauhaus as a continuation of the work in Germany where he becomes director.

Mies van der Rohe emigrates to the US and becomes head of IIT in Chicago.

1939

Mies van der Rohe starts designing the IIT Campus in Chicago which completes in 1958.

1951

Mies van der Rohe designs the Seagram Building in New York City.
